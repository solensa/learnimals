Executive Summary

Research findings on temporary workspace design and its impact on productivity and wellbeing in construction projects.

Research Overview

Study: "The site office – an exploration into how temporary workspaces can be designed to maximise productivity and wellbeing"

Author: Chris Rose, 2024

Focus: Relationship between leadership priorities and user experiences in temporary office facilities on construction projects

Key Research Findings

Methodology:

  • Interviews with 6 key leaders from 3 similar construction projects
  • Survey of 128 office users using Building Use Studies methodology
  • Thematic analysis of responses across comparable temporary office facilities

Three Critical Success Factors

1. Working Environment Infrastructure

Internet data supply, meeting rooms, and environmental conditions are fundamental to productivity

2. Noise Management & Focus

Background noise affects concentration; balance needed between collaboration spaces and focused work areas

3. User Control & Flexibility

Users often lack control over their environment; traditional practices and budget constraints limit customization

Current Challenges

One-size-fits-all, cost-driven approach neglects evolving user needs in technology-driven work environments

Business Impact & Implications

Current State: Project leadership teams recognize the importance of efficient, diverse workspaces but are constrained by traditional practices and budgets.

Opportunity: Improving facility specification processes could significantly enhance user satisfaction and productivity.

User Profile: Knowledge workers (engineers, managers) require environments that balance collaboration with focused work in increasingly technology-driven roles.

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Invest in Technology Infrastructure: Prioritize robust internet connectivity and digital collaboration tools
  2. Design for Flexibility: Create spaces that can adapt to different work modes and team configurations
  3. Noise Management Strategy: Implement acoustic solutions and zone planning to minimize distractions
  4. User-Centered Planning: Involve end users in facility design and specification processes
  5. Challenge Traditional Practices: Re-evaluate standard approaches to better meet evolving workforce needs

Research Significance

This study advances knowledge in the under-explored field of temporary office supply for construction projects. The findings provide evidence-based insights for improving workplace design decisions that directly impact employee satisfaction and project productivity.

Next Steps: Organizations should review their current temporary workspace strategies and consider implementing user-focused design processes to maximize return on workspace investments.
